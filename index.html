// WHY GENERIC ECOMMERCE BREAKS

Builders merchant commerce is not a normal catalogue problem.

A merchant storefront has to understand branches, account pricing, call-off orders, regulated products, heavy delivery, click-and-collect, credit limits, and the gap between counter trade and online self-service.

01
ERP and web data drift apart

Product, price, stock, customer terms, and invoices live in systems that were never designed for fast online buying.

02
Trade accounts need context

One customer can have multiple buyers, branches, budgets, addresses, approval rules, and negotiated terms.

03
Delivery is operationally complex

Bulk items, split consignments, local fleets, timed drops, and branch collection need rules at checkout, not manual calls later.

// COMMON QUESTIONS

What we would cover on the first call.

Do we need to replace everything at once?

No. The best starting point is usually the workflow causing the most cost: price and stock sync, trade account ordering, branch availability, checkout delivery rules, or catalogue quality.

Can this work with our ERP?

Yes. The accelerator is designed around adapter-based integration, so SAP, Microsoft, Kerridge, Epicor, in-house platforms, and other ERP patterns can be mapped without direct database dependency.

Where does Hyva fit?

Hyva provides the fast, maintainable storefront foundation. Scandiweb adds the merchant-specific workflows, Magento architecture, integration layer, analytics, and growth programme around it.
